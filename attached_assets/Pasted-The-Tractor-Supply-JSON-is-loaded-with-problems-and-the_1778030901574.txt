The Tractor Supply JSON is loaded with problems — and they're more subtle than just "wrong numbers." A few stand out:
The smart-looking lies. UC-01's revenue formula displays as "0% × $14.8B × 0.95 × 0.75 = $15.8M". That's not 0%, it's 0.15% — the display is rounding to integer percent and producing a math statement that reads as 0 × anything = $15.8M, which is gibberish on its face. A CFO will see "0%" and either (a) think the model is broken or (b) catch you and stop trusting anything.
The cap warnings are the tell. UC-04, UC-07, UC-08 all show "Revenue uplift capped from 95% to 5%". Discover originally proposed that an automated reporting suite would lift Tractor Supply's revenue by 95%. The validator caught it and clamped to 5% — which is still wildly wrong; an internal reporting tool generates ~0% revenue uplift, the value is in cost and risk. The cap is a band-aid on hallucination, not a fix.
The aggregate doesn't pencil. $134.9M annual value on $14.8B revenue = 0.91% of revenue, 12% of net income, from ONE year of an AI program. IRR of 346.8% with 0-month payback. NPV of $343.7M. These are the kind of numbers that get a deal killed in 90 seconds when the CFO does back-of-envelope math.
Risk exposure is hand-waved. $566M of "Risk Exposure" summed across 10 use cases with no documented basis. A merchandising forecast tool claiming $80M of exposure reduction needs to point to a specific category of loss (markdown, stockout, working capital) tied to a real KPI baseline, not a vibe.
The cash flow math is dimensionally wrong. UC-04: 14,000,000 × (90 / 365) × 0.08 × 0.85 × 0.75 = $176K. The "$14M" is described as "Revenue at Risk" but the formula uses it as if it's working capital tied up in inventory. Wrong base. The cash flow benefit formula should be Revenue × (Days Improved / 365) × Cost of Capital × Realization × Maturity — using Annual Revenue like UC-01 does correctly. UC-04 mixed up "Revenue at Risk" and "Annual Revenue" and produced a number that looks plausible but is calculated against the wrong base.
Tokens are off by 3-5x in the realistic direction, low. 1,200 input + 300 output per run for a multi-agent forecasting system that orchestrates pattern recognition + outlier detection + forecast synthesis across 3 years of POS data is nonsense. Real agentic patterns on Claude burn 15K-40K input tokens per run with system prompts, tool schemas, retrieved context, and intermediate reasoning. Discover is undercounting tokens dramatically, which means Atlas's downstream infrastructure sizing is going to be wrong.
